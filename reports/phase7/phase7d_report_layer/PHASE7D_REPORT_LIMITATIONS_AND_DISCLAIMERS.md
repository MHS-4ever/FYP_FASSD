# Phase 7D — Limitations and Disclaimers

Standard text blocks for every forensic report. The 7D1 builder must include **all mandatory items** plus any status-specific additions.

---

## 1. Mandatory disclaimer (full paragraph)

Use verbatim in `disclaimer` unless locale translation is approved:

> **Disclaimer:** This report was generated by the FASSD forensic decision-support prototype. It combines automated acoustic models, chunk-level scores, and rule-based calibration outputs. It is intended to **assist** human review only and must **not** be treated as the sole basis for legal, disciplinary, employment, or law-enforcement action. Results do not constitute proof of authenticity, deception, identity, or intent. A qualified reviewer should evaluate the original recording, context, and chain of custody before any consequential decision.

---

## 2. Mandatory limitations (bullet list)

Include all of the following in `limitations[]` (order may vary):

1. **Prototype system:** This is a decision-support prototype, not a certified or market-validated forensic product.  
2. **Decision layer only:** Final statuses come from Phase 7C4-v2 fusion rules over multiple model outputs, not a single end-to-end classifier.  
3. **Clean human uncertainty:** On the Phase 7C1 controlled set, most nominally clean human files were classified as **borderline** (manual review), not automatically accepted.  
4. **Direct AI file-level gap:** File-level direct-AI detection is not fully solved; **segment-level evidence** is used when file-level scores are weak.  
5. **Evaluation scope:** Models and rules were primarily evaluated on **controlled Phase 7C1** and **Phase 7A** test suites, not all real-world channels, devices, languages, or codecs.  
6. **No legal proof:** Outputs must not be presented as legal proof, court-ready conclusions, or guaranteed detection.  
7. **Expert review:** High-stakes use requires **manual expert review** of audio and metadata.  
8. **Data gaps:** More external, multi-device, multi-language, and platform-compressed data is needed before broad deployment claims.  
9. **Attack typing:** Multiclass attack hints are auxiliary and may be unreliable on processed or replayed audio.  
10. **Environmental factors:** Compression, noise, and recording chain strongly affect scores; environmental features are indicative only.

## 3. Status-specific limitations (append when applicable)

| `calibrated_status` | Additional limitation |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| `clean_human_borderline` | Borderline means **conflicting evidence**; the file must not be labeled synthetic without expert listen. |
| `clean_human_false_alarm` | Known **false-alarm risk** on clean human speech under current thresholds. |
| `direct_ai_file_level_missed_but_segment_suspicious` | File-level pooled vote may be REAL while segments remain suspicious—review timeline. |
| `partial_fabrication_not_evaluable` | Partial-region metrics were not computed due to missing or invalid suspicious timestamps. |
| `partial_fabrication_missed` | Partial fabrication may be present but not detected under current chunk rules. |
| `*_missed` (evaluation) | Internal evaluation flagged a possible miss; external context may still warrant review. |
| `is_error_case=true` | Listed in Phase 7C4-v2 error-case set for controlled evaluation. |

> Performance on Urdu, Pakistani accents, and phone-recorded chains may differ from studio English benchmarks. Domain-specific validation is required before operational use.

## 7. What reviewers should tell stakeholders

| Say | Do not say |
|-----|------------|

| “Manual review required.” | “The system cleared it.” |
| “Segment X–Y shows elevated spoof-like scores.” | “Only the middle is AI.” (absolute) |
| “Human-origin with replay risk.” | “Definitely human and original.” |
